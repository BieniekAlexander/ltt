Polish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Etymology 1[edit]

Inherited from Old Polish imie\xc4\x87, himie\xc4\x87, jemie\xc4\x87, jmie\xc4\x87 (ymyecz, ymecz, ymyal, yym\xc4\x85l, gimyal, gymene, gymyecz, gymyal, nye gmyal)[1][2], from Proto-Slavic *j\xd1\x8cm\xc4\x9bti (stem *j\xd1\x8cma-).

Verb[edit]

mie\xc4\x87 impf (frequentative miewa\xc4\x87)

  1. (transitive) to have
    Mam kota. \xe2\x80\x95 I have a cat.
  2. (intransitive) to be (for an age)
    Mam 22 lata. \xe2\x80\x95 I am 22 [years old]. (literally I have 22 years.)
  3. (auxiliary, intransitive) must, have to, need to, supposed to
    Mamy i\xc5\x9b\xc4\x87 na zakupy. \xe2\x80\x95 We have to go shopping.
  4. (intransitive) to feel something
  5. (reflexive) to fare (to be in any state, or pass through any experience, good or bad)
  6. (reflexive) to consider oneself
    Masz si\xc4\x99 za lepszego? \xe2\x80\x95 You think you\'re better than me?
  7. (intransitive, impersonal, strictly in the negative) to be gone, to not be present, there is no.
    Antonym: by\xc4\x87
